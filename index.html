Let us see a table regarding changing the tenses:


Tense Simple Continuous Perfect Perfect Continuous
Past Rule: Verb + ed (or irregular form)
Example: I played.		 Rule: was/were + verb-ing
Example: I was playing.		 Rule: had + past participle
Example: I had played.		 Rule: had been + verb-ing
Example: I had been playing.
Present Rule: Verb (add -s for he/she/it)
Example: I play.		 Rule: am/is/are + verb-ing
Example: I am playing.		 Rule: have/has + past participle
Example: I have played.		 Rule: have/has been + verb-ing
Example: I have been playing.
Future Rule: will + verb
Example: I will play.		 Rule: will be + verb-ing
Example: I will be playing.		 Rule: will have + past participle
Example: I will have played.		 Rule: will have been + verb-ing
Example: I will have been playing.

Words that I learnt from the story

Floating: Not fixed; not living permanently in one place.
Sentence: The boat was floating gently on the lake.

Chatting: Talking in a casual or informal way.
Sentence: They were chatting happily at the cafe.

Swirl: To move in a twisting or spiralling pattern.
Sentence: The leaves began to swirl in the wind.
